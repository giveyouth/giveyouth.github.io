Executive Team

Makda Dejene

Makda is a senior at Justice High School and has been working with GIVE since her freshman year. She started as a tutor and later served as a center manager for Whitefield Commons CRC. This is her second year as an Executive Director, where she focuses on organizational tasks and maintaining relationships with center managers and others within GIVE. Makda enjoys being an Executive Team member because it allows her to help in solving the educational inequity in her community that she has been personally affected by. In her free time, she loves to read, bake, spend time with her family, and find new hobbies.

Nikita Rao

Nikita is a senior at Westfield High School. She has been a part of GIVE Youth since her freshman year in high school. She was initially a tutor at the Chantilly Center, then the Chantilly Center Manager, and now this is her first year serving as an Executive Director. Nikita is in charge of the responsibilities of the publicity and social media aspects of GIVE. She is passionate about the mission of GIVE and wants to provide everyone, no matter what background they come from, help in order to succeed in school. She is also very dedicated to protecting the environment and solving global issues. Some of Nikita’s hobbies include playing volleyball, listening to music, traveling, cooking, and spending time with her family and friends.

Bill Chen

Bill is a senior at West Springfield High School and has been part of GIVE Youth since his sophomore year of high school. He initially started as a tutor at Pohick Regional Library, and then joined the core team the summer before his junior year. This is his first year as an Executive Director and he is in charge of maintaining community outreach efforts and helping expand the organization to more students in underserved areas. Coming from a low-income background himself, Bill is passionate about GIVE’s mission and enjoys serving as a member of the Executive Team because it allows him to use his own background to help with the growth of GIVE and help students find a means of affordable educational resources. Outside of GIVE, Bill enjoys playing the violin and piano, hiking, binge watching YouTube videos, spending time with friends and family, and trying new foods.

Mahika Rawat

Mahika Rawat is a senior at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology and is excited to be a part of the Executive Team this year. She has been working with GIVE since her freshman year and is passionate about their mission to serve underprivileged students to ensure equal educational opportunities throughout the community. She started off by tutoring at the Chantilly Regional Library, proceeded to be a Center Manager at the Centreville Regional Library for two years, and is now an Executive Director. Mahika is in charge of publicity and social media for GIVE. Along with this mission, Mahika is interested in pursuing a career in the medical field. Outside of GIVE, Mahika is involved in social justice groups and enjoys classical Indian dance, swimming, traveling, and hanging out with friends and family

Mustafa Lonandwala

Mustafa Lonandwala is a senior at Oakton High School and has been a part of GIVE since his freshman year. He started off as a tutor at the Chantilly Regional Library for two years and then worked on the core team as a website developer during the summer of his sophomore year. This is his first year as an Executive Director where he will be leading GIVE’s technological initiatives and managing financial tasks. Mustafa is dedicated to bridging the educational gap in low income communities by providing free tutoring services and materials and organizing outreach efforts. He believes that by being an Executive Director at GIVE, he will be able to make a positive impact on his community. Mustafa also believes in the importance of STEM education and is passionate about applying computer science to social justice improvement. Outside of GIVE, Mustafa is interested in music theory and plays the piano, enjoys going on long hikes, and reading anything John Green related.

Tina Gao

Tina is a Junior at Oakton High School and has been working with GIVE since her freshman year. She has tutored both at the Chantilly regional library and at the Pohick Center, and has also worked on the resource committee on the core team. Tina works on the Outreach aspect of GIVE by maintaining and forming partnerships with local community organizations. She enjoys being a part of GIVE because it gives her the opportunity to help others, and she hopes to help expand GIVE’s services as an executive director. Outside of GIVE, Tina loves to cook, travel, and watch crime related shows.
